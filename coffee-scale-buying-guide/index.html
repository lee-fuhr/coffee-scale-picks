How to choose a coffee scale

Updated June 2026Independently researchedNo paid placement.

Quick answer

A coffee scale helps you measure beans and water precisely for consistent brews. Look for 0.1g resolution, at least 500g capacity, a built-in timer, and some water resistance. Avoid guessing with scoops, a scale is the single best upgrade for better coffee at home.

Why do you need a coffee scale?

Brewing coffee without a scale is like baking without a measuring cup. The difference between a good cup and a great one often comes down to the ratio of coffee to water. Using a scale lets you dial in your dose exactly, repeat your favorite recipes, and adjust if something tastes off. A coffee scale removes the guesswork from your morning routine. Whether you use a pour-over, French press, or espresso machine, consistent weight measurements help you reproduce that perfect brew every day. Once you start weighing, you’ll wonder how you ever managed without it.

What resolution and capacity do you need?

Resolution is the smallest weight the scale can reliably register. For coffee, 0.1g resolution is the sweet spot, it’s sensitive enough to measure single beans or small adjustments, but fast enough for daily use. Coarser scales with 1g resolution are too jumpy for pour-over dosing. Capacity matters because you’ll often weigh both coffee and water. A scale that handles at least 500g works for most single-cup brewers; 1000g or more is helpful for big carafes or batch brew. Most dedicated coffee scales sit in this range without being oversized.

Should your coffee scale have a built-in timer?

Yes, a timer is almost essential for pour-over and other manual methods. You need to track brew time, how long the water is in contact with the coffee, to adjust grind size and pour speed. A scale with an integrated timer keeps both functions in one device, so you’re not juggling a phone stopwatch. Many scales start the timer automatically when they detect the first pour. That convenience makes it easy to focus on your technique instead of pressing buttons. If you mainly use an espresso machine or automatic dripper, you might not need a timer as much, but it’s still handy for dialing in.

How water-resistant does your coffee scale need to be?

Pour-over brewing means you’ll occasionally spill hot water on the scale. A basic splash-resistant design (often a silicone mat over the surface) protects the electronics from drips. Some scales are fully waterproof, meaning you can rinse them directly under the tap. For home use, a water-resistant scale with a sealed battery compartment is usually enough. Fully waterproof models are nice for cleaning but cost more and may have other trade-offs. Avoid non-coated electronics if you plan to use a scale in a busy coffee bar environment.

What other features are worth considering?

Auto-tare is standard on any good coffee scale, it lets you zero out the weight of your container in one touch. Look for a scale that re-tares quickly so you can add coffee, then water without fuss. A backlit display or a bright LCD helps readability in dim kitchen light. Size is important if you have limited counter space. Slim, compact scales fit under a brewer spout more easily. Battery life varies widely: some scales run for months on a coin cell; others require frequent recharging. Choose based on how often you’re willing to swap batteries or plug in a USB cable.

Which coffee scales are worth your money in 2026?

There’s a reliable coffee scale for every budget. Budget-friendly options (roughly under a few hundred dollars) from well-known brands like Hario and Timemore deliver 0.1g resolution, a timer, and water resistance, everything a home brewer needs. These are simple, durable, and widely recommended by experts and owners. Mid-range scales from Brewista or the latest from Acaia add conveniences like an app companion, faster response times, and gorgeous build quality. Pro-level models (several hundred dollars) offer extreme accuracy, automatic flow-rate tracking, and rugged waterproofing. Your choice should match how serious you are about dialing in recipes and whether you value data tracking or just consistent results.

Frequently asked questions

Can I use a regular kitchen scale for coffee?

Yes, but most kitchen scales only read to 1g, which is too coarse for precise coffee dosing (you want 0.1g). Also, they usually lack a timer and are less water-resistant. A dedicated coffee scale makes brew ratios much easier to replicate.

What does 0.1g resolution mean for coffee?

0.1g resolution means the scale can measure changes as small as one-tenth of a gram. This lets you dose exactly the number of beans you want and adjust your recipe in tiny increments, which is especially helpful for single-serve pour-over and espresso.

Do I need a waterproof coffee scale?

Not necessarily, splash-resistant is usually enough for home use. Waterproof scales are easier to clean and more durable in busy settings, but they cost more. If you often brew with a wet kettle or rinse your scale under the tap, go waterproof.

How important is the timer on a coffee scale?

For pour-over methods, it’s very important because brew time directly affects extraction. A built-in timer keeps you from fumbling with a phone or stopwatch. If you make espresso or use a fully automatic machine, a standalone timer or phone app may suffice.

What is the ideal capacity for a coffee scale?

500g capacity covers most single-cup brewing (coffee plus water). For a full Chemex or large French press, look for 1000g or more. Most dedicated coffee scales are designed within this range, check your largest brewer’s capacity before buying.

Should I get a scale with auto-tare?

Absolutely. Auto-tare lets you zero the scale with your container on it so you can add ingredients one at a time. It’s a basic convenience, and almost all coffee scales include it. Make sure the scale re-tares quickly after you add coffee, before you pour water.

In shortChoosing the right coffee scale comes down to matching features to your brewing style. Focus on 0.1g resolution, a built-in timer, and enough capacity for your largest vessel. A splash-resistant body and a responsive display make daily use a pleasure. Skip unnecessary frills if they don’t help you brew consistently, your taste buds will thank you.
